# terms-and-conditions
Privacy Policy for Consent OS
Last Updated: April 22, 2026
1. Introduction:
  Consent OS is designed to return control of personal data to the user. We are committed to transparency and do not sell or monetize your information. This policy explains how our extension interacts with your data to provide the Consent Dashboard and revocation features.
2. Information We Access:
   To fulfill its purpose as a centralized management layer, Consent OS accesses the following:
     Personally Identifiable Information: To display which of your accounts are linked to third-party services.
     Web History & Activity: To build your Permission History Timeline and track when consents were granted.
     Location Data: To visualize which services are tracking your physical coordinates.
     Website Content: To parse and translate complex legal agreements into plain language for better transparency.
  
3. How We Use Your Data
   Visualization: We map data flows to show you which organizations have access to your info.
   Management: We facilitate the "One-click revocation" of permissions you no longer want.
   Education: We simplify legal jargon to help you make informed decisions.
4. Data Storage & Security
     Local Storage: All analyzed data and dashboard preferences are stored locally on your device.
     No Third-Party Sharing: We do not sell, trade, or transfer your data to outside parties. Our only goal is to reduce the risk of data leakage and abuse.
5. Your Rights
   You have the absolute right to view, manage, and revoke access to your data at any time through the Consent OS interface.
